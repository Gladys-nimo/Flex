Intorduction to Engineering

Intorduction:





Biochemical Engineering: Biochemical engineering studies combine chemical engineering and microbiology topics to introduce students to the field of microbial systems. These courses traditionally cover subjects such as enzyme kinetics, microbial growth modeling, fermentation systems and enzyme engineering.
Foundations of Software Engineering: Online graduate-level courses of this nature provide students with a comprehensive overview of current software development methods relevant to engineering. Subjects tend to include component-based software, such as C# and .NET, algorithms related to modeling, analysis and visualization and basic software maintenance.
Foundations of Software Engineering: Graduate students considering a career teaching college-level science and engineering programs are the primary audience for this course, which is usually delivered via online interactive seminar format. Instruction covers creating questions for tests and homework, methods for teaching equations and techniques for delivering interesting lectures. These courses are commonly offered at an introductory level that's suitable for students with or without teaching experience.
Aerospace Engineering:This course is designed to teach students the fundamentals of aerospace engineering and development. Students will learn about systems such as spacecraft and airplanes, from design to build. Topics include aeronautic technology, dynamics and engineering risks.
Entrepreneurship for Engineers: In this online course, students can learn how to turn an engineering idea into a successful business. Topics include marketing, financing, product development, strategic partnership opportunities and how to evaluate business ideas.
Sustainable Energy: Sustainability is the focus of this course, which explores advances in energy extraction systems and energy usage. It also looks at how mathematical, engineering and market factors play a role in the development of sustainable energy infrastructure.




Intorduction to Civil Engineering


Civil Engineering Design: Students learn the design process for civil engineering projects, which involves hand-sketching, applying computer-aided design (CAD) and choosing appropriate materials, tools and techniques. Consideration for outside factors, such as the economic and environmental impact of engineered structures, is emphasized in these online engineering courses.
